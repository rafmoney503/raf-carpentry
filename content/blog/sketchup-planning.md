## Why Every Carpenter Should Learn SketchUp

When I first picked up SketchUp, I was skeptical. I'm a carpenter — I work with wood, not computers. But within a week, I realised this tool was going to change everything about how I approach a project.

## The Problem With Traditional Planning

Before SketchUp, my planning process looked like this: rough sketches on paper, measurements scribbled in a notebook, and a lot of mental arithmetic. It worked, but it was slow and error-prone. One wrong measurement could mean wasted materials and lost time.

## How SketchUp Changed My Workflow

Now, every project starts with a 3D model. I can:

- **Visualise the finished product** before cutting a single piece of wood
- **Take exact measurements** from the model for my cut list
- **Show clients** exactly what they're getting — no surprises
- **Spot problems** before they happen on the workshop floor
- **Export to Cabinetos** for automatic parts lists and board optimisation

## Getting Started

If you're a carpenter thinking about learning SketchUp, start with the free version. Watch a few YouTube tutorials on woodworking-specific techniques. Within a few days, you'll be modelling basic cabinets. Within a month, you'll wonder how you ever worked without it.

## The Bottom Line

SketchUp isn't just a nice-to-have — it's a competitive advantage. Clients trust you more when they can see exactly what you're building. And you'll save time, materials, and stress on every single project.
